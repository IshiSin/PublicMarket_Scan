# Alphabet 2025-Q4 — AI Key Takeaways

1. **$175B–$185B AI infrastructure capex planned for 2026.** Alphabet disclosed it will invest $175 billion to $185 billion in 2026 to scale AI compute capacity for frontier model development, Cloud, Search, and Other Bets.

2. **GPU and TPU supply chain partnerships expand compute options.** Google will be among the first to offer NVIDIA’s Vera Rubin GPU platform and continues to deploy its seventh-generation Ironwood TPUs, maintaining what it calls the industry’s widest variety of AI compute choices.

3. **Gemini 3 drives 3× higher token throughput vs. prior generation.** Since launch, Gemini 3 Pro has averaged 3× as many daily tokens as Gemini 2.5 Pro, and first-party Gemini models now process over 10 billion tokens per minute via direct API use, up from 7 billion last quarter.

4. **78% reduction in Gemini serving unit costs in 2025.** Alphabet lowered inference costs by 78% over 2025 through model optimizations, efficiency gains, and better utilization while scaling Gemini across products and APIs.

5. **Google Cloud AI revenue accelerates nearly 400% YoY.** Revenue from products built on Google’s generative AI models (Gemini, Imagen, Veo, etc.) grew nearly 400% year-over-year in Q4, with ~350 customers each processing more than 100 billion tokens in December.

6. **8 million paid Gemini Enterprise seats sold to 2,800+ companies.** Launched four months ago, Gemini Enterprise has surpassed 8 million paid seats across more than 2,800 organizations and managed over 5 billion customer interactions in Q4, up 65% YoY.

7. **Compute constraints expected to persist through most of 2026.** Executives said demand for AI compute exceeds available supply and Alphabet expects to operate in a “supply-constrained way” through much of 2026 as new data center capacity ramps.

8. **~60% of CapEx allocated to servers; 40% to data centers and networking.** In 2025 and expected again in 2026, about 60% of Alphabet’s CapEx went to servers (AI compute) and 40% to long-duration assets including data centers and networking equipment.

9. **Google Antigravity agent platform reaches 1.5 million weekly active users in two months.** Powered by Gemini, Antigravity enables autonomous agents to plan and execute complex software tasks and surpassed 1.5 million weekly active users shortly after launch.

10. **Waymo hits 20 million fully autonomous trips and 400,000 weekly rides.** Waymo surpassed 20 million fully autonomous trips in December, provides more than 400,000 rides weekly, and is expanding to new U.S. cities plus the U.K. and Japan.
